Oatmeal started the way most great companies do: with two founders, a shared
inbox, and an unread count that had quietly passed four thousand. We were not
trying to build a support tool. We were trying to make the notifications stop.

## What we believe

Support is the last place most companies invest and the first place their
customers notice. We think that's backwards. Every reply is a product decision,
and every ignored thread is a churn forecast that hasn't filed its paperwork yet.

So we built the inbox we wanted: one place for every conversation, enough
context to answer without reading the whole thread, and automation that handles
the parts nobody misses.

## How we work

We're a small, deliberately unglamorous team spread across six time zones. We
ship on Fridays, we read our own support queue, and we keep our roadmap in the
open — mostly so our customers can tell us when we're wrong, which they do.

If any of that sounds like a place you'd like to work, our careers page has the
details, and our inbox is, predictably, always open.
